The essentials on ePTO, savings, and getting WorkForce onto your trucks.

An ePTO is a battery-powered system that runs auxiliary truck equipment — bucket lifts, cranes, digger derricks — with the diesel engine fully off. A traditional PTO requires the engine to idle for the entire job; an ePTO operates that equipment silently from an onboard battery, eliminating fuel use, engine wear, and worksite idling.

A WorkForce-equipped bucket truck typically saves $9,000–$16,000 per truck, per year in fuel and maintenance. A single truck eliminates roughly 1,890 gallons of diesel and 42,300 lbs of CO₂ annually. Payback usually lands between 24 and 36 months — and under 18 months when federal, state, and utility incentives are stacked.

Yes. It's a parallel system that retrofits onto in-service utility, telecom, and municipal work trucks regardless of make, model year, or chassis manufacturer. Installation typically takes under two days, and the original PTO remains operational as a backup.

The battery is sized to power a full shift — typically six to eight hours of continuous bucket or hydraulic operation. Options range from 14.4 kWh for standard operations to 28 kWh for high-duty-cycle work, and it recharges overnight on a standard 120V depot outlet.

Because it's a parallel system, the truck's original engine-driven PTO stays fully operational as a backup. If the battery is depleted — or in any emergency — the operator can switch to the conventional PTO instantly with no downtime, and the truck completes the job.

Yes. It qualifies as idle-reduction technology under EPA's DERA grants and most state clean-fleet programs. Combined federal, state, and utility incentives can cover 30%–80% of project cost in many jurisdictions.
